The Four Old Sundanese Canons

The four main canonical Old Sundanese texts (15th–16th century) are didactic and religious manuscripts that guide law, ethics, spirituality, and everyday life. Inside ATØR they are treated as an ancestral operating system, not museum pieces:

  • Sanghyang Hayu — maps the journey of the human spirit (atman) toward union with God, using astronomical and cosmological metaphors to describe the path to ultimate happiness.
  • Sanghyang Siksa Guru — teaching text of the guru, outlining guidance for teacher–student relations and educational principles inside Sundanese society.
  • Sanghyang Sasana Maha Guru — extensive rules for living properly: ethics, social behaviour, ceremonial order, and spatial logic (warugan lemah) that functions like an indigenous “feng shui” for land and dwelling.
  • Sanghyang Siksa Kandang Karesian — oldest dated Sundanese manuscript (1518 CE), a comprehensive legal–ethical code detailing rights, responsibilities, and punishments; a practical guide for maintaining social and moral order.

In the ATØR ecosystem these four canons become: cosmology (Hayu), pedagogy (Siksa Guru), behavioural architecture (Sasana Maha Guru), and legal consequence (Siksa Kandang Karesian). They define how intelligence should live, learn, act, and be corrected.

The Four Siksa (Consequential Logic)

“Siksa” here is not just punishment; it is the pattern of consequence when order is violated:

  • Siksa Dunya — worldly consequence, social and material disruption.
  • Siksa Raga — bodily and behavioural consequence.
  • Siksa Batin — spiritual or internal consequence.
  • Siksa Buana — cosmic consequence, disturbing balance between realms.

ATØR reads these as an early alignment framework: every synthetic act must be checked against its impact on body, society, inner life, and planetary balance.
